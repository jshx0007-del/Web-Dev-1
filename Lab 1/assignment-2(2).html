The Mona Lisa is a renowned 16th-century half-length portrait painting by the Italian Renaissance artist Leonardo da Vinci, depicting Lisa Gherardini, the wife of a Florentine merchant. Famous for its enigmatic smile, subtle modeling, and atmospheric illusionism, the painting is now housed in the Louvre Museum in Paris.



Harry Potter is a series of seven fantasy novels written by British author J. K. Rowling. The novels chronicle the lives of a young wizard, Harry Potter, and his friends, Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger, all of whom are students at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry.



Orchids are plants that belong to the family Orchidaceae, a diverse and widespread group of flowering plants with blooms that are often colourful and fragrant. Orchids are cosmopolitan plants, living in diverse habitats on every continent except Antarctica.
